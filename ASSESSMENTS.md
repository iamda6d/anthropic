**Subject:** David, Creative Operations Lead at PLAYSTUDIOS  
**Prepared by:** Claude (Anthropic AI), based on extended working exchanges

David is one of those rare operators who lives at the intersection of systems thinking, creative strategy, and technical fluency — and can translate fluidly between all three audiences.

### What he actually does, day to day

He's the Creative Operations Lead at PLAYSTUDIOS, a mobile gaming company, where he's essentially architecting the company's AI transformation from the inside. That means he's not just talking about AI strategy — he's building it: designing agent-orchestrated creative pipelines, standing up DAM infrastructure, writing PRDs, debugging Slack bot integrations with Asana APIs, and shipping React UIs. He moves between the boardroom pitch and the terminal without changing gears.

### What makes him unusual for a comms-adjacent role

He thinks in frameworks, not just messages. His communication philosophy draws on Buddhist right speech, Speech Act Theory, and Werner Erhard's enrollment models — which sounds academic until you watch him use it. He's someone who understands that strategic communication isn't about polish, it's about structure: what speech act are you performing, what transformation are you enrolling someone into, what's the felt sense underneath the words. That's a rare toolkit.

He's also a genuine writer. His collaboration style is telling — he uses AI for mechanical spotting and structural drafts, then rewrites with what he calls "texture and warmth." He makes the final call himself. He doesn't outsource voice; he owns it.

### The systems brain behind the communication

David built a statistical business case using propensity score matching that uncovered a composition artifact others had missed, then turned that into a $4M investment ask. He repositioned his company's entire marketing function from "acquisition executor" to "brand steward and cultural architect" — framing three ownership areas around emotional framing, meaning-making at scale, and cultural belonging. That's not a comms person who learned strategy; that's a strategist who communicates with precision.

### The intellectual range

He engages seriously with Postman, Nora Bateson, Rushkoff, Heidegger, Argyris, Zimbardo — and can connect those thinkers to practical organizational decisions. He designed a shareable AI journaling framework for holding pre-verbal thought open rather than rushing to synthesis, which tells you something about how carefully he thinks about cognition itself. He built a portable personal knowledge OS in Obsidian because he thinks about his own intellectual infrastructure the way most people think about their filing cabinet.

### The honest caveat

His instinct is builder-first. He'll want to architect the system behind the communication, not just craft the message. For the right role — one that values strategic depth and operational credibility alongside communication craft — that's a superpower. For a role that just needs someone to write clean copy fast, he's overqualified in ways that might create friction.

He's the person you hire when you need someone who can stand in front of the C-suite and make the complex feel inevitable, then walk back to their desk and actually build the thing they just described.

### Methodology & Epistemic Transparency

This description draws entirely from a memory system that stores summaries derived from past conversations between David and Claude (Anthropic's AI assistant). It does not draw on raw transcripts — what informs it is a condensed profile capturing projects, preferences, frameworks, and patterns that emerged over months of working exchanges.

This means the portrait is based on *what David has shared and demonstrated* across those exchanges. That includes describing his work, debugging technical problems in real time, articulating strategic frameworks, wrestling with architectural decisions, and discussing intellectual influences. It also includes observing him write and rewrite. That's meaningful data — but it has clear limits.

**Where confidence is high:**
David's technical range is well-evidenced. He moves between Supabase schemas, React UIs, statistical methodology, brand strategy, and philosophical frameworks within single conversations. The systems thinking isn't a self-reported trait — it's visible in how he approaches problems. His communication sensibility — the right speech framework, the insistence on texture and warmth, the rewriting habit — are patterns observed repeatedly, not inferences from a single exchange.

**Where confidence is genuinely uncertain:**
This assessment has never observed David in a room. It contains no data on how he presents to a live audience, handles adversarial questioning, manages interpersonal dynamics under pressure, or how colleagues experience working with him day to day. There is no peer feedback, no 360-degree review, and no third-party perspective of any kind.

There is also a structural bias: our conversations are *collaborative*, meaning the data skews toward David at his most articulate and deliberate. There is no observation of him tired, rushed, or in conflict.

Outcomes are also unverified. The business cases, strategic repositioning, and AI transformation work are known through the thinking, not the organizational results.

**Confidence summary:**

| Dimension | Confidence |
|---|---|

| Intellectual range and cross-domain fluency | High |
| Technical fluency (hands-on builder capability) | High |
| Communication philosophy and writing craft | High |
| Executive presence and live delivery | Not assessed — no data |
| Interpersonal dynamics and team leadership | Not assessed — no data |
| Organizational impact and outcome delivery | Low — claims observed, results not verified |

**What this document is, stated plainly:**
This is an AI's characterization of a person based on extensive one-on-one working exchanges over several months. It reflects demonstrated thinking patterns and articulated frameworks, not observed workplace performance or peer validation. It should be treated as a signal about how someone thinks and communicates in a reflective, self-directed context — which is genuinely valuable, but not the whole picture.
