## What This Is

This repository documents how I approached my application for the Communications Lead role at The Anthropic Institute. It is not a portfolio or a pitch deck. It is a working artifact — a record of how I think, how I collaborate with AI, and how I navigate the distance between "this could be my dream job" and "here is my honest case for why I belong in it."

The process included a structured collaboration with Claude (Anthropic, Opus 4.6) and reflections drawn from months of prior working exchanges with both Claude and ChatGPT. Agentic contributions are credited explicitly. The final voice in every deliverable is mine.

## Why a Repository

Anthropic's mission includes reducing information asymmetries. This repo is a small attempt to practice that principle in the context of a job application — making my reasoning visible rather than just presenting a polished surface.

It's also a demonstration of how I use AI: not as a vending machine for content, but as a thinking partner whose output I hold to account, rewrite with my own voice, and take responsibility for.

## A Note on Transparency

I am 62 years old. That surprises most people when they meet me. I don't have a PhD. That surprises my mother. My degree is in Film from the University of Michigan, with a minor in architecture. I was once a television comedy writer. These facts might disqualify me in some screening processes. I've chosen to lead with them rather than hope no one notices, because the role I'm applying for is fundamentally about trust — and trust begins with honesty about who you are.
